About Us

Who We Are

Global Solidarity Uganda is a non-profit organization dedicated to addressing critical community challenges through sustainable, inclusive, and innovative approaches. We work with communities to improve livelihoods, build resilience to climate change, and empower vulnerable groups through skills development and practical solutions.

Vision

To build resilient, self-reliant, and empowered communities living in harmony with sustainable environmental practices.

Mission

To transform communities through sustainable innovations, skills development, and inclusive empowerment programs that address climate change, improve livelihoods, and promote social equity.

Core Values

  • Sustainability: Promoting environmentally friendly solutions
  • Inclusiveness: Leaving no one behind
  • Innovation: Encouraging practical and creative solutions
  • Empowerment: Building self-reliance and dignity
  • Integrity: Transparency and accountability in all actions
  • Collaboration: Working with communities and partners

Aims

  • Improve livelihoods through income-generating activities
  • Equip communities with practical and marketable skills
  • Promote environmentally friendly technologies
  • Empower vulnerable and marginalized groups
  • Enhance food security and nutrition

Objectives

  • Train youth and women
  • Promote renewable energy
  • Improve farming practices
  • Increase access to clean water
  • Support climate action
  • Strengthen innovation

Key Activities

  • Community training and sensitization
  • Skills development and vocational training
  • Demonstration projects and pilot innovations
  • Community mobilization and outreach programs
  • Technology development and promotion
  • Environmental conservation initiatives
  • Support for small-scale enterprises

Programs & Projects

  • Food security & value addition
  • Renewable energy innovations
  • Metal fabrication & tin smithing
  • Water solutions
  • Clay works & construction materials
  • Sanitation services
  • Smart agro innovations (aquaponics, hydroponics)
  • Sustainable farming practices
  • Vocational skills development

Target Beneficiaries

  • Youth
  • Women
  • Persons with Disabilities (PWDs)
  • Refugees and displaced communities
  • Indigenous groups (e.g., Batwa)
  • Smallholder farmers

Our Approach

Community-based participation, hands-on training, local resource use, innovation, sustainability, and scalability.

Why Work With Us

Strong community engagement, practical low-cost solutions, sustainability focus, inclusive programs, and proven innovation experience.
